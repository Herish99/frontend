Async & await

Certainly! async and await are modern features in JavaScript that make working with asynchronous code more readable and organized, especially when dealing with Promises. These keywords provide a more synchronous-like style to handle asynchronous operations. Let's break it down with a simple example:

Imagine you're at a food truck, and you want to order a sandwich. Instead of standing in line and waiting for your turn, you take a numbered ticket and wait until your number is called. When your number is called, you can go directly to the counter to get your sandwich.

In this analogy:

  • Taking a Numbered Ticket (Async): You initiate the order (asynchronous operation) and are given a numbered ticket (Promise) that represents the future result.
  • Waiting for Your Number (Await): You use the await keyword to wait for your number (Promise) to be called. This allows you to pause your actions without blocking other people's orders.
  • Getting Your Sandwich (Result): When your number is called and your sandwich is ready (Promise is fulfilled), you directly go to the counter to get your sandwich (get the resolved value).
